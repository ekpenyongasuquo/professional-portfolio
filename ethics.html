Ethical Dilemma Analysis

During my academic experience, I encountered an ethical dilemma involving the handling of sensitive system access credentials during a technical project. A peer suggested sharing login credentials to save time and complete the task faster.

While this approach appeared efficient, it violated basic cybersecurity principles, institutional policies, and professional ethics. Sharing credentials increases the risk of unauthorized access, data breaches, and accountability issues.

I chose to follow ethical and professional standards by refusing to share credentials and instead encouraged proper access controls and documentation. This decision reinforced the importance of integrity, responsibility, and adherence to security policies.

From this experience, I learned that ethical decision-making often requires courage and patience. Upholding professional standards protects not only systems and data, but also trust, reputation, and long-term success in the IT field.
